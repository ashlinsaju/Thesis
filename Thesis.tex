\subsection*{Abstract}


Among the risk factors for serious health issues, air pollution ranks the highest annually, accounting for many deaths and contributing to chronic diseases. The City of Prince George has reported poor air quality over the years due to forest fires, road dust, geographical location, and other factors. The conventional method for monitoring air quality uses stationary monitoring systems installed by government agencies. These may not be effective as they are usually few in number. To achieve the goal of public awareness and engagement, pollution monitoring must become accessible for everyone. This goal appears to be achievable due to the advances in electronics, low-cost computing, and networking. In our thesis project, we have created an air pollution monitoring system using low-cost sensors and open-source software to visualize the data. This project can serve as a template for end-users or communities interested in understanding the air quality in their local area.
